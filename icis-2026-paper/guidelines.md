# ICIS 2026 Paper Guidelines & Checklist

## Conference Details
- **Conference:** 47th International Conference on Information Systems (ICIS 2026)
- **Theme:** "Digital Collaboration and Coexistence"
- **Location:** Lisbon, Portugal | December 13-16, 2026
- **Submission Deadline:** May 1, 2026
- **Target Track:** Track 1 — Digital Collaboration and Coexistence (Theme Track)

## Paper Title
**"Architects of Intent: Designing Governable Human-AI Coexistence in Agentic Service Systems"**

## Formatting Requirements (Desk Rejection Triggers)
- [ ] **16 pages max** (single-spaced) for completed research — title, authors, abstract, keywords, and references are EXCLUDED from the count; all text, figures, tables, and appendices ARE included
- [ ] **8 pages max** for short papers (same rules)
- [ ] **150-word abstract** or less
- [ ] Use the **official ICIS 2026 template** on Overleaf / from the ICIS website
- [ ] Submit as **Adobe PDF only**
- [ ] **NO author names anywhere** — not in the body, abstract, headers, footers, or PDF metadata (double-blind review). Remove document properties too.
- [ ] Anonymize self-citations: replace "we previously showed (Author, 2026)" with "prior work has shown (Author, 2026)" — do not signal it is your own work
- [ ] Do NOT exceed page limits — instant desk rejection

## Reviewer Evaluation Criteria (What Gets Scored)
1. **Importance of research objectives** — is this a meaningful, timely problem?
2. **Relevance and completeness of theory** — is the theoretical lens appropriate?
3. **Appropriateness of methodology** — does the method match the research question?
4. **Quality of analysis** — are analyses applied correctly and systematically?
5. **Strength of evidence** — do findings support the claims made?
6. **Clarity of writing** — well-organized, logical flow, no ambiguity
7. **Audience appeal** — will this draw interest at the conference?
8. **Track relevance** — does it fit "Digital Collaboration and Coexistence"?
9. **Literature review** — appropriate, complete, well-situated in IS discourse

## Top Rejection Reasons to Avoid
1. **Weak/unclear contribution** — the #1 killer. State it explicitly in the introduction. "Gap-filling" alone is NOT enough — explain why filling the gap produces novel insight.
2. **Reads as "Part 2" of another paper** — the ICIS paper MUST stand alone. Reference the AMCIS paper as prior work, but the contribution must be independently valuable.
3. **Methodological weakness** — method must be rigorous and match the research question. ICIS expects journal-level quality.
4. **Overstated claims** — only claim what your data supports.
5. **Poor track fit** — ensure framing aligns with "coexistence" and "collaboration" themes.
6. **Insufficient theoretical grounding** — need a clear theoretical lens, not just a framework from the prior paper.

## Strategic Positioning (How This Paper Relates to the AMCIS Paper)

### What the AMCIS paper did:
- Conceptual artifact analysis of RPA vs. agentic AI through BPM lifecycle lens
- Identified "lifecycle collapse," accountability gap, deskilling paradox
- No empirical data — called for validation in its limitations section

### What THIS paper must do differently:
- **Standalone contribution** — new theoretical or design knowledge that the AMCIS paper could not produce
- **Empirical or design science grounding** — real system, real data, real evaluation
- **New insight** — phenomena, principles, or theory that go BEYOND confirming the AMCIS propositions
- **Frame around coexistence** — how do humans and agentic AI coexist in service processes? What design principles make this work?

### Contribution Framing (Strong vs. Weak)

| Weak (will be rejected) | Strong (ICIS-worthy) |
|---|---|
| "We validate the lifecycle collapse" | "We derive design principles for governing agentic service systems" |
| "We implement the conceptual model" | "We surface new tensions in human-AI agency that conceptual work could not anticipate" |
| "We test the deskilling paradox" | "We develop theory on procedural literacy erosion in agentic contexts" |

## Recommended Research Approach
**Design Science Research (DSR)** is the strongest fit because:
- The AMCIS paper identified *problems* (accountability gap, deskilling paradox)
- DSR lets you design *solutions* (governance artifacts, oversight mechanisms)
- The Theme Track explicitly welcomes "design science and intervention research"
- DSR produces design principles = standalone, actionable contribution

Alternative approaches: case study in a real organization deploying agentic automation, or a controlled experiment on the deskilling paradox.

## Writing Quality Checklist
- [ ] Contribution stated explicitly in the introduction (within first 2 pages)
- [ ] Research questions are precise and answerable
- [ ] Theoretical background justifies the lens used (not just describes it)
- [ ] Method section is detailed enough for replication
- [ ] Findings are presented with evidence, not just assertions
- [ ] Discussion distinguishes findings from interpretation
- [ ] Implications are concrete for both researchers and practitioners
- [ ] Limitations are honest and specific
- [ ] No filler, no padding — every paragraph earns its space in 16 pages
